Welcome aboard. The first week isn't about being perfect — it's about proving to yourself that a habit can be small, pleasant, and genuinely doable. Here's how we'd spend it.

<Callout icon="🐣" title="Before you start" tone="accent">
Pick just **one** habit for week one. One. We know it's tempting to overhaul your whole life — resist it. You'll add more once the first one feels effortless.
</Callout>

## The week, day by day

<StepList>
  <Step number={1} title="Days 1–2 · Make it laughably small" body="Shrink your habit to something you can't fail. One page, one push-up, one glass of water. Check it off and let yourself feel the win." />
  <Step number={2} title="Days 3–4 · Anchor it" body="Attach your habit to something you already do. Let Pip set the nudge for right after your existing routine — coffee, teeth, laptop-close." />
  <Step number={3} title="Days 5–7 · Protect the streak, gently" body="Busy day? Use a grace token, not guilt. The goal this week is showing up — quality and quantity can grow later." />
</StepList>

## What "good" looks like

You do not need a perfect seven-for-seven week. If you checked in most days and enjoyed it, you've already succeeded. Here's the honest bar:

<Checklist>
  <Check text="You kept the habit tiny enough to do on a bad day" />
  <Check text="You checked in at least four of the seven days" />
  <Check text="You used a grace token instead of quitting after a miss" />
</Checklist>

<FigureGallery>
  <Figure variant="nudge" caption="Set a nudge for the moment you're most likely to say yes." />
  <Figure variant="streak" caption="Watch the streak grow — grace tokens keep it alive." />
</FigureGallery>

<PullQuote>
Week one isn't about results. It's about becoming someone who shows up.
</PullQuote>

That's it. Do this for seven days and you'll have something most people never get: proof that change can be gentle. Next week, you get to add habit number two — but that's a story for another guide.
